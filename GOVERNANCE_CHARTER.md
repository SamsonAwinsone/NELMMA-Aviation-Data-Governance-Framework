## 1. Purpose

This Data Governance Charter formally establishes the authority, structure, responsibilities, and operating principles of the Nelmma Aviation Data Governance Program. It serves as the foundational governing document for all data management activities across the organization.

---

## 2. Scope

This charter applies to:
- All data created, collected, processed, or stored by Nelmma Aviation
- All employees, contractors, partners, and third parties who access Nelmma data
- All operational markets: Ghana, Nigeria, Liberia, Mali, China, Belgium
- All business functions: cargo operations, passenger services, customs, finance, HR, and executive aviation

---

## 3. Governance Objectives

The Data Governance Program is established to:

1. Define clear ownership and accountability for all data assets
2. Ensure compliance with applicable data protection laws (Act 843, GDPR, PIPL)
3. Improve data quality across all operational domains
4. Enable secure, auditable, and ethical data use
5. Support business scalability and international expansion
6. Build and maintain stakeholder trust through transparent data practices

---

## 4. Governance Principles

| Principle | Description |
|---|---|
| **Accountability** | Every data asset has a named owner responsible for its quality and appropriate use |
| **Integrity** | Data is accurate, complete, and maintained to the highest quality standards |
| **Transparency** | Data practices are documented, auditable, and communicated to stakeholders |
| **Security** | Data is protected proportionate to its sensitivity and regulatory requirements |
| **Privacy** | Individual privacy rights are respected in accordance with applicable law |
| **Proportionality** | Only data necessary for stated business purposes is collected and retained |
| **Continuity** | Governance processes persist through organizational change |

---

## 5. Governance Structure

### 5.1 Data Governance Council
- **Chair:** Chief Executive Officer
- **Members:** Heads of Cargo, Passenger Services, Finance, IT, HR, and Legal/Compliance
- **Frequency:** Quarterly strategic meetings; extraordinary meetings as required
- **Authority:** Approve governance policies; resolve cross-domain data disputes; set data strategy

### 5.2 Data Governance Lead
- **Reports to:** CEO / COO
- **Responsibilities:** Day-to-day program management; policy development; audit coordination; training oversight; compliance reporting

### 5.3 Data Owners
- **Appointed by:** Governance Council
- **Scope:** Accountable for data quality and appropriate use within their business domain
- **Domains:** Cargo & Freight | Passenger Records | Customs & Compliance | Finance | HR | Partner Data

### 5.4 Data Stewards
- **Appointed by:** Data Owners
- **Scope:** Operational responsibility for data quality, metadata accuracy, and policy adherence

### 5.5 Data Custodians
- **Role:** IT and systems staff responsible for secure data storage, backup, and technical access controls

### 5.6 Data Protection Officer (DPO)
- **Mandate:** Required under Ghana Act 843 and GDPR
- **Responsibilities:** Monitor legal compliance; handle data subject requests; liaise with regulatory authorities

---

## 6. Decision Rights

| Decision Type | Decision Maker |
|---|---|
| Adopt new governance policy | Governance Council (majority vote) |
| Assign Data Owner to a new domain | Governance Council |
| Approve data-sharing agreements | DG Lead + Legal/DPO |
| Classify a new data asset | Data Owner (with DG Lead sign-off) |
| Escalate a data quality issue | Data Steward → Data Owner → DG Lead |
| Report a data breach | DG Lead → CEO → Regulatory Authorities |
| Modify access permissions | DG Lead + IT Custodian |

## 8. Review & Amendment

- This charter is reviewed **annually** by the Governance Council
- Amendments require approval from the CEO
- All versions are stored in the governance repository with change logs
- Major amendments are communicated to all staff via training updates

---

## 9. Enforcement

Violations of this charter or its subordinate policies may result in:
- Mandatory retraining
- Disciplinary action per HR policy
- Regulatory notification (where legally required)
- Suspension of data access privileges
